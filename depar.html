The Department of Wildlife Conservation in Sri Lanka serves as a crucial guardian of the nation's biodiversity, overseeing the protection of diverse ecosystems within national parks and wildlife reserves. Balancing the needs of local communities with the imperative of conservation, the department employs scientific expertise, technology, and community engagement to mitigate human-wildlife conflicts, protect endangered species, and promote habitat restoration. Their efforts play a vital role in preserving Sri Lanka's unique natural heritage, fostering a sustainable coexistence between humans and the rich array of wildlife that inhabits the country.
