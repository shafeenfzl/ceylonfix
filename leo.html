Exploring Sri Lankan Leopard Habitats

A Glimpse into Three Conservation Hotspots

Yala Park

Home to Sri Lankan leopards, Yala's diverse landscapes in the southeast provide an ideal habitat for these elusive big cats.

Wilpattu Park

The largest park in Sri Lanka, Wilpattu's northwest location features natural lakes "villus" and is vital for leopard conservation.

Horton Plains Park

Horton Plains houses leopards amidst montane grasslands and cloud forests, showcasing their adaptability to diverse ecosystems.

Leopard Conservation Challenges in Sri Lanka

Understanding Threats and Conservation Efforts

1. Poaching

Illegal Exploitation

Poaching poses a severe threat to leopards in Sri Lanka, involving illegal hunting for their skins and body parts, leading to population decline. Conservation efforts focus on strengthening law enforcement and community engagement.

Explore
2. Habitat Loss

Urbanization Impact

Rapid Urbanization and agricultural expansion contribute to the loss of leopard habitats, leading to increased human-wildlife conflict. Conservation strategies include habitat restoration projects and community awareness.
