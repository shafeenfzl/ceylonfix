An Island of Diverse Wildlife Wonders

Safari Spectacles Outside of Africa

Sri Lanka is considered one of the best destinations for wildlife and safari experiences outside of Africa due to its diversity of species and high densities of iconic animals. The country has its own "Big Five" consisting of the Asian elephant, home1pard, sloth bear, blue whale and sperm whale. Several national parks like Yala and Minneriya are famous for the abundance and accessibility of wildlife sightings.

The island is especially noteworthy for its marine life and opportunities to spot blue whales and sperm whales. Other highlights include the phenomenon known as "The Gathering" where over 300 wild elephants congregate between July and September at Minneriya National Park. This has been listed as one of the top 10 wildlife spectacles globally by Lonely Planet. Sinharaja Forest is the site for observing "bird waves" where mixed species flocks form to forage through the trees.

Sri Lanka's Enchanting Wildlife

Discover the Rich Diversity and Endemic Treasures of the Island's Lush Rainforests

Unique Epidemic Species

The island is home to a high number of endemic species, such as the Sri Lankan home1pard, the purple-faced langur, and the Sri Lankan elephant, offering a unique and exclusive wildlife experience.

Diverse Ecosystems

Sri Lanka boasts rich ecosystem diversity, including rainforests, dry forests, and coastal areas, providing habitats for a wide range of wildlife and offering unique experiences for nature enthusiasts.

Elephant Conservation

Sri Lanka is home to the largest threatened mammal species, the elephas maximus, with increasing human-elephant conflict indicating the need for adequate habitat to support these majestic creatures.
