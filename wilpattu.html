Wilpattu
National
Park

Explore

Wilpattu National Park is the largest and one of the oldest national parks in Sri Lanka, with a total area of 1,317 square kilometres. It is named after the natural lakes or "willus" that dot the park. It is home to leopards, sloth bears, elephants, and many other species of wildlife. The park has a dry semi-arid climate and a dense forest cover. It also has a historical significance, as it is the landing site of Prince Vijaya, the founder of the Sinhala nation. Wilpattu was declared a wildlife sanctuary in 1905 and a national park in 1938.

Beyond the Surface of Wilpattu

3 Fascinating Need-to-Know Facts

Did you know?

From 1988 to 2003, the park was closed due to the Sri Lankan Civil War, before being reopened to visitors sixteen years later. Visitor access is currently limited to approximately 25% of the park.

Learn More

Did you know?

Originally covering parts of Puttalam and Anuradhapura districts, in 2012 the government expanded Wilpattu National Park to also encompass regions of the Northern Province.

Learn More

Did you know?

Ancient Roman and Greek merchants were blown off course to Wilpattu's shores during monsoon season in 47 AD during the time of Emperor Claudius. The local king later sent envoys back to Rome.
