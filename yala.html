Yala
National
Park

Explore

Yala National Park is the most visited and second largest national park in Sri Lanka, with a total area of 979 square kilometres. It is famous for its high density of leopards, elephants, and aquatic birds. The park has a variety of ecosystems, from moist monsoon forests to freshwater and marine wetlands. It also has some ancient Buddhist sites, such as Sithulpahuwa and Magul Vihara. Yala was declared a wildlife sanctuary in 1900 and a national park in 1938.

Did you know?

Yala National Park is composed of metamorphic rock from the Precambrian era and has a flat and mildly undulating topography.

Did you know?

Yala National Park has a pilgrimage site that about 400,000 pilgrims visit annually. It is the landing site of Prince Vijaya, the founder of the Sinhala nation.

Did you know?

The Park is divided into five blocks to maintain a pristine area in the face of tourism and other activity. Blocks 1 and 5 are for the public to visit. Blocks 2, 3 and 4 are more rugged and require permits to enter.
