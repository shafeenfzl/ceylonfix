Minneriya National Park

Whispers of History and Echoes of Nature: A Timeless Sri Lankan Jewel

A Gathering Place for Giants

Minneriya becomes the stage for an incredible phenomenon, a mass gathering of elephants. Hundreds emerge from the surrounding forests to bask in the retreating waters of the Minneriya reservoir, forming herds of up to 300 individuals. The sheer concentration of these highly social creatures in one area creates a majestic sight during the months of July through October.

Safaris ride through the shrub forests and grasslands along the reservoir's shore, glimpsing intimate elephant behaviors that are not always visible when ranging solitary in the wilds. Yet along with the elephants, Minneriya remains home to spotted deer, sambar deer, buffalo, wild boar and 24 species of reptiles through the dry months.

As water eventually returns during the rainy season, the elephant gatherings dissipate back into the broad reaches of Minneriya National Park where they feed and shelter year round.

Horton Plains National Park

Where the Grassland Meets the Cloud Forest: Sri Lanka's Misty Mountain Gem

A Highland Wilderness

Horton Plains features a unique montane grassland ecosystem, with highland plateaus over 2,000 meters in elevation. The cool climate supports grasslands interspersed with stunted cloud forests rather than dense jungle.

Despite its small size, Horton Plains hosts an impressive diversity of flora and fauna. Over 20 mammal species roam the plains, including sambar deer, purple-faced langurs, and the rare rusty-spotted cat. Endemic bird species include the dull-blue flycatcher and Sri Lanka white-eye. Many rare plants are found here too, especially orchids and rhododendrons.

Nearly half the birds and four of the mammals found in Horton Plains are endemic species, highlighting its vital role in Sri Lankan biodiversity conservation. Ongoing efforts like patrolling for illegal activity help protect this ecosystem and its inhabitants. Carefully managed tourism provides revenue supporting conservation.
